# Why Now - The 3 Convergence Factors

The governance vacuum didn't exist two years ago. Three things happened in 2025-2026 simultaneously that created it - and a 12-18 month window to capture the category before it closes.

---

## Factor 1: Microsoft Created the Vacuum

**What happened:** Microsoft launched Entra Agent ID (2025, now GA) and Agent 365 (GA May 2026 at $15/user/month). These are the first enterprise-grade tools to give AI agents first-class managed identities.

**What they missed:** Every enterprise that evaluates Entra Agent ID immediately hits the same three walls:
- *"What about our non-Microsoft agents?"* - Agent 365 is weak on Copilot Studio alternatives, LangChain, n8n, Salesforce Einstein
- *"What about HRIS integration?"* - Entra has no Workday connector for HR-driven lifecycle
- *"What about agents owned by people who left?"* - No cascade deprovision, no orphan cleanup

Pedigree answers all three. The best time to sell a complement is when the category is being defined.

**The timing insight:** Microsoft's launch created enterprise awareness of the problem - CISOs are now having the "agent governance" conversation. But Microsoft's solution leaves the hardest gaps unsolved. We walk into a warm conversation.

---

## Factor 2: ServiceNow/Veza Acquisition Creates Churn

**What happened:** ServiceNow acquired Veza for $1 billion (December 2025, now completed). Veza was the leading independent agent governance / NHI access management platform.

**Why this matters:**
- Veza customers are uncertain about product roadmap post-acquisition
- ServiceNow is absorbing Veza into a $150B platform - existing Veza sales relationships are disrupted
- CISOs who were evaluating Veza are now reconsidering
- The $1B acquisition validates the category to investors and to enterprise buyers

**The window:** There is typically a 12-18 month period after a major acquisition where the acquired product's customers are vulnerable to alternatives. That window is open now.

**What Pedigree offers:** A sharper, more specific story than Veza had. Veza was broad access graph + NHI discovery. Pedigree is purpose-built for the agent governance problem with the HRIS lineage angle nobody else has.

---

## Factor 3: Compliance Deadlines Are Real

**What's arriving:**
- **SOX** - public companies with AI agents making financial decisions will face auditor scrutiny. "We're figuring it out" stops working.
- **HIPAA enforcement on AI** - agents accessing patient data are under increasing regulatory attention
- **EU AI Act** - enterprise AI governance requirements are now law in the EU, affecting multinationals
- **SEC guidance on AI** - emerging guidance on disclosure and governance for AI systems in financial workflows

**The CISO's problem:** Right now, most enterprise security teams cannot answer basic audit questions about their agent fleet:
- How many agents do you have? (Most don't know)
- Who owns each one? (Spreadsheets at best)
- What happened when Sarah from the finance team left? (Her agents are probably still running)

**Pedigree's answer:** A complete audit bundle, exportable for SOX/HIPAA/regulator review, generated automatically from the lineage graph. The compliance story isn't a feature - it's an unlock for enterprise procurement.

## The Window: 12-18 Months

The category will have a winner within 18 months. Here's why:

1. Microsoft will eventually close some of these gaps natively. Not all of them - the HRIS integration problem is harder than it looks, and Microsoft doesn't own Workday - but they will make incremental progress.

2. Another well-funded startup will raise $20-50M on this thesis. The $1B Veza exit is a bright green signal to every enterprise security VC.

3. Enterprises that have deployed AI agents at scale will start to have governance failures - orphaned agents causing breaches, compliance incidents, audit failures. The market will accelerate.

**The action:** Build the Wesco PoC, get to $1.5M ARR, raise on the proof. The category is real, the timing is right, and the unique angle (HRIS-native lineage graph) is unoccupied.
