## TAM: Non-Human Identity Access Management

**The headline number:** $12.2 billion in 2026 -> $38.8 billion by 2036 at approximately 12% CAGR.

*Source: Yahoo Finance / Market Research Report, 2026*

This market includes all tooling for managing machine identities: service accounts, API keys, certificates, bot credentials, and increasingly - AI agents. The AI agent governance sub-category is the fastest-growing segment, driven by the explosive deployment of autonomous agents in enterprise environments.

## Analyst Coverage

### Gartner - 2026 IAM Predictions (January 2026)
- Identity visibility for non-human identities is "no longer optional"
- IAM leaders must act as human and machine identities have emerged as the primary attack surface
- AI agent governance is listed as a top priority capability gap

### Fortune / Okta - "AI Agents Are Acting Like Employees" (April 2026)
Key quote: *"The governance frameworks executives built over decades were designed for people. AI agents are not people, and the gap between those two facts is where enterprise risk is now accumulating fastest."*

- 91% of organizations are already using AI agents
- Only 10% have a clear strategy to manage them
- "The real threat in enterprise AI adoption is not how intelligent agents are, but how much authority executives delegate to them"

### Forbes - "Agent Registries Become The New Battleground For Cloud Giants" (April 2026)
- AWS, Microsoft, and Google all launching agent registries simultaneously
- Each hyperscaler approaching from a different angle - none owns the governance primitive
- "As organizations move from experimental agents to production fleets, whoever controls the discovery and governance layer stands to shape how enterprises manage their AI operations for years"

### Strata.io - "The AI Agent Identity Crisis" (2026)
- 23% of organizations have a formal, enterprise-wide strategy for agent identity management
- Agent identity has no clear owner in most enterprises
